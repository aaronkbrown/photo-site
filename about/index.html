About David Brown

Hi, and thanks for visiting!

I am an amateur photographer living in the Pacific Northwest. I enjoy all kinds of photography, from wildlife to macro. I shoot with a Canon 7D Mark II crop frame and a Canon 6D full frame.

I acquired my first two cameras with my paper route money when I was in 8th grade—a Speed Graphic 2¼ x 3¼ and a Yashica 35mm SLR. I learned to develop my own film, so my father and I built an enlarger for printing. When Acufine came on the market, I learned to push my Tri-X to ASA 1600 and got great sports shots for my high school yearbook.

In my early 20s, I got a Minolta of some kind and began processing color slides. I also tried my hand at color prints with some limited success, but then photography got away from me.

I later married a woman who had a Canon AE-1 and kind of took it over from her, but we just took pictures of our children as they grew up.

When digital arrived, I went through a series of point-and-shoots but was always looking for a sharper image. In January 2013, I broke down and bought a Canon Rebel, and I've been thoroughly engrossed in photography since then.

I hope you enjoy looking at my pictures as much as I've enjoyed taking them!
